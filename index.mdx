PharmaVault is a full-stack web application that helps individuals and families manage their pharmaceutical inventory. Built with ASP.NET Core Blazor (Interactive Server) and PostgreSQL, it provides a secure, real-time dashboard to monitor medication stock levels, track expiration dates, and maintain a master catalog of drugs — so you never run out of a critical medicine or keep expired stock on hand.

## What PharmaVault Does

PharmaVault solves a common household problem: managing personal or family medical supplies often leads to expired medications, unnecessary duplicate purchases, and lost track of prescriptions. PharmaVault centralizes all of this into a single, secure web interface.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Inventory Tracking" icon="boxes-stacked">
    Add, edit, and delete medication stock linked to your user account. Each item tracks quantity, purchase date, expiration date, and prescription notes.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Expiration Alerts" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    The dashboard automatically categorizes stock into Good Condition, Expiring Soon (within 30 days), and Expired — with visual color coding.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Medicine Catalog" icon="book-medical">
    Maintain a master catalog of drugs with their pharmaceutical form (tablet, capsule, syrup) and dosage. All inventory items link back to the catalog.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Getting Started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set up PostgreSQL">
    Install PostgreSQL locally or via Docker, then run the two SQL initialization scripts to create the database and schema.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure your connection string">
    Use .NET User Secrets to store your PostgreSQL credentials securely without committing them to source control.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run the application">
    Build and launch the Blazor app with `dotnet run` or `dotnet watch` for hot reload during development.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Register and log in">
    Create your user account through the registration page. Your password is hashed with BCrypt before storage.
  </Step>
</Steps>
